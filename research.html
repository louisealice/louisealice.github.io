The Impact of Dynamic Prices on Electric Vehicle Public Charging Demand: Evidence from a Nationwide Natural Field Experiment

with Andy Hackett, Robert D. Metcalfe, Luca Panzone, and Andrew Schein

NBER Working Paper No. 34600

A nationwide natural field experiment with approximately 110,000 EV public-charging users, identifying the elasticity of charging demand to dynamic prices. Findings informed Octopus Energy's launch of an equivalent dynamic-pricing product in the French market.

Decarbonizing Heat: The Impact of Heat Pumps and a Time-of-Use Heat Pump Tariff on Energy Demand

with Andy Hackett, Robert D. Metcalfe, and Andrew Schein

NBER Working Paper No. 33036 — Resubmitted to American Economic Journal: Economic Policy

Field-experimental evidence on how heat pump adoption and a dedicated time-of-use tariff reshape household electricity demand, drawing on half-hourly smart meter data from a UK natural field experiment. Cited in policy work including the Hendren et al. Policy Impacts Library.

2023

Nowhere Else to Go? Urbanisation and Flood Risks in China: The Role of Land Scarcity

with Paolo Avner, Jun Rentschler, and Capucine Riom

World Bank — Global Facility for Disaster Reduction & Recovery

Causal evidence on how land scarcity drives urbanisation into flood-exposed areas, combining large-scale geospatial data with quasi-experimental identification. Conducted under the World Bank Sustainable Development Fellowship.

2023

The Impact of Segregated Cycling Lanes on Road Users

SSRN Working Paper No. 4353625

Event-study analysis of the Cycle Superhighways roll-out in central London (2014–2019), finding that segregated lanes raised cycling traffic by about 25% on opening and a further 20% per year, with a substantial fall in cyclist accidents and no detectable impact on car traffic.
